# Dubai — SkyNest Destination Guide

## Overview
Dubai is the city of superlatives — the world's tallest building, the largest shopping mall, man-made islands shaped like a palm tree. Once a fishing village, it is now a global hub for business, luxury, and increasingly, culture and adventure. SkyNest's most popular destination.

## Hub Airport
**Dubai International (DXB)** — Terminal 2 for SkyNest arrivals. One of the world's busiest airports.

---

## Best Time to Visit
| Season | Months | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Peak / Ideal | Nov – Mar | 20–28°C; outdoor activities comfortable; Dubai Shopping Festival (Dec–Jan) |
| Shoulder | Apr, Oct | Warm but manageable |
| Off-peak / Hot | May – Sep | 35–45°C; mostly indoor activities; lowest hotel prices |

---

## Must-See Attractions

**Icons**: Burj Khalifa (observation decks At The Top and At The Top SKY), Palm Jumeirah, Burj Al Arab exterior
**Shopping**: Dubai Mall (world's largest), Mall of the Emirates (indoor ski slope), Gold Souk, Spice Souk in Deira
**Culture**: Al Fahidi Historic District (old Dubai), Dubai Frame, Museum of the Future
**Outdoors**: Desert safari (4x4 dune drive, camel ride, BBQ dinner under stars), Hatta mountain pools, Kite Beach
**Theme Parks**: Dubai Parks & Resorts (Legoland, Motiongate), IMG Worlds of Adventure

---

## Neighbourhoods
- **Downtown Dubai**: Burj Khalifa, Dubai Fountain, high-end dining
- **Dubai Marina**: Waterfront living, JBR beach walk, nightlife
- **Deira / Bur Dubai**: Old city, souks, affordable stays
- **Palm Jumeirah**: Luxury resorts, private beaches, Atlantis
- **Business Bay**: Corporate hub, boutique restaurants

---

## Getting Around
- **Dubai Metro**: Red and Green lines cover major tourist areas; ~AED 3–8 per trip
- **Taxis**: Metered, affordable (~AED 10 flagfall); Careem app available
- **SkyNest Car Rental**: Available at DXB terminal; see Dubai Cars section

---

## Currency
UAE Dirham (AED). USD 1 ≈ AED 3.67 (pegged). ATMs everywhere. Cards widely accepted. Cash useful for souks.

---

## Practical Tips
- Dress modestly in souks and religious sites; swimwear only on beaches and pools
- Ramadan: restaurants may be closed daytime; alcohol restricted during Ramadan month
- Friday/Saturday is the UAE weekend — some government services closed
- Tipping: 10–15% at restaurants is appreciated (not mandatory)
