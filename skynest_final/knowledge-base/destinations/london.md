# London — SkyNest Destination Guide

## Overview
London is one of the world's great capitals — a city of 2,000 years of history where ancient Roman walls stand beside glass skyscrapers, where free world-class museums line the Thames, and where every neighbourhood feels like a different city. SkyNest connects India, Dubai, Singapore, Paris, New York, and Bangkok directly to London Heathrow.

## Hub Airport
**London Heathrow (LHR)** — Terminal 3 for SkyNest arrivals. Heathrow Express to Paddington: 15 minutes, £25. Tube: ~45–60 min, £6 (Oyster card).

## Best Time to Visit
| Season | Months | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Summer (Peak) | Jun – Aug | Warm (18–25°C), long days; festivals, outdoor events; most expensive |
| Spring | Mar – May | Lovely (cherry blossoms in parks), increasingly busy |
| Autumn | Sep – Nov | Cool, beautiful foliage; fewer crowds |
| Winter | Dec – Feb | Cold but festive (Christmas lights, markets); cheapest hotels |

---

## Must-See Attractions

**History**: Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, Westminster Abbey, St Paul's Cathedral, Greenwich Royal Observatory
**World-Class Free Museums**: British Museum, Natural History Museum, V&A Museum, Tate Modern, National Gallery, Science Museum
**Neighbourhoods**: Shoreditch (street art, tech), Notting Hill (colourful townhouses, Portobello Market), Soho (dining, nightlife), South Bank (Thames walk, food market)
**Entertainment**: West End theatre (Book of Mormon, Les Misérables, Hamilton), Ronnie Scott's jazz club, Camden Market
**Day Trips**: Bath (Roman baths, Georgian architecture), Oxford/Cambridge, Cotswolds villages, Windsor Castle

---

## Getting Around
- **London Underground (Tube)**: 11 lines, covers almost everything; buy Oyster card or use contactless
- **Buses**: Double-decker iconic; contactless payment
- **Elizabeth Line**: Fast route across the city (Heathrow to Paddington in 15 min)
- **Black cabs**: Metered; hail on street or use the Gett app
- **Uber**: Available and reliable
- **SkyNest Car Rental**: Available at LHR Terminal 3; note: driving on the left; Congestion Charge in central London (£15/day)

---

## Currency
British Pound (GBP). USD 1 ≈ GBP 0.79 (2026). Cards universally accepted; many places are now cashless.

---

## Practical Tips
- UK uses 240V electricity; bring UK plug adapters (Type G, 3-pin)
- Stand on the right on escalators; walk on the left
- Service charge (12.5%) often added to restaurant bills; check before tipping additionally
- Pub culture: ordering at the bar is the norm; no table service in traditional pubs
