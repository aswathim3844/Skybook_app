# Tokyo — SkyNest Destination Guide

## Overview
Tokyo is a city of extraordinary contradictions — ancient shrines in the shadow of neon towers, Michelin stars in basement ramen shops, silence in Buddhist temples a block from the world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Japan's capital is one of the safest, most efficient, and most endlessly fascinating cities on Earth.

## Hub Airport
**Tokyo Narita International (NRT)** — Terminal 1 for SkyNest arrivals. Narita Express (N'EX) to Shinjuku/Tokyo station: 60 minutes, ¥3,070. Limousine Bus to major hotels: ¥3,600.

## Best Time to Visit
| Season | Months | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Spring (Cherry Blossom) | Late Mar – Apr | Most popular; extraordinary beauty; book 4+ months ahead |
| Autumn (Foliage) | Nov | Red and gold foliage; equally stunning; less crowded than spring |
| Summer | Jun – Aug | Hot and humid; festivals (Obon); some great fireworks |
| Winter | Dec – Feb | Cold but festive; illuminations; Christmas–New Year |

## Must-See Attractions

**Iconic Tokyo**: Shibuya Crossing (world's busiest), Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa), Meiji Shrine, Tokyo Skytree (634m view), teamLab Planets (digital art)
**Neighbourhoods**: Shinjuku (neon, Golden Gai), Harajuku (fashion, Takeshita Street), Akihabara (electronics, anime), Shimokitazawa (vintage, indie music), Yanaka (old Tokyo, temple cats)
**Food**: Tsukiji outer market (breakfast sushi), Ichiran Ramen (24/7 solo booth ramen), Depachika (department store basement food halls), Omakase sushi (reserve months in advance)
**Day Trips**: Nikko (ornate shrines, mountain scenery, 2 hours), Hakone (Mt Fuji views, hot springs, 90 minutes), Kamakura (Great Buddha, temples, 1 hour), Kyoto (cultural heartland, 2.5 hours by Shinkansen)

## Getting Around
- **IC Card (Suica / Pasmo)**: Load credit and tap on any train, subway, bus; also works at convenience stores and vending machines
- **Tokyo Metro + Toei Subway**: Near-perfect coverage of central Tokyo; very frequent
- **JR Yamanote Line**: Loop line connecting major areas (Shinjuku, Shibuya, Harajuku, Akihabara, Tokyo, Ueno)
- **Shinkansen (Bullet Train)**: From Tokyo station to Kyoto (2h 15m, ¥14,170), Osaka, Hiroshima
- **Taxis**: Metered, honest, expensive (~¥700 flagfall)
- **SkyNest Car Rental**: Available at NRT; note: driving on the left; International Driving Permit required

## Currency
Japanese Yen (JPY). USD 1 ≈ JPY 150 (2026). Cash still widely used — many smaller establishments card-free. Convenience store ATMs (7-Eleven, Japan Post) accept foreign cards.

---

## Practical Tips
- Quiet on trains — no phone calls; eating discouraged on most lines
- Bow slightly when thanked; remove shoes before entering homes and some traditional restaurants (tatami rooms)
- Rubbish bins are rare — carry your waste until you find one (konbini have bins)
- Get a pocket Wi-Fi or SIM at Narita airport (essential for navigation)
- 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson: arguably the world's best convenience stores
