# Bangkok — SkyNest Destination Guide

## Overview
Bangkok is a city that overwhelms all the senses simultaneously — golden temple spires above a tuk-tuk exhaust haze, the smell of pad thai from a street cart, neon signs reflecting in the Chao Phraya river at night. Thailand's capital is one of the world's top tourist destinations and the gateway to the kingdom's beaches, jungles, and ancient ruins.

## Hub Airport
**Bangkok Suvarnabhumi International (BKK)** — Terminal for SkyNest: International Arrivals, Level 2. Airport Rail Link to central Bangkok: 30 minutes, THB 45. Taxi: metered (~THB 200–350 to most hotels + THB 75 expressway tolls). Grab: ~THB 250–500 to city.

| Season | Months | Notes |

## Must-See Attractions

**Grand Palace & Temples**: Grand Palace + Wat Phra Kaew (Emerald Buddha) — must book online; Wat Pho (giant reclining Buddha, world-famous massage school); Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn — sunrise and sunset)
**Culture & Markets**: Chatuchak Weekend Market (10,000 stalls, every product imaginable), Maeklong Railway Market, Damnoen Saduak Floating Market, Khao San Road (backpacker hub)
**Modern Bangkok**: ICONSIAM riverfront mall (indoor floating market), Asiatique The Riverfront (night market + Ferris wheel), MahaNakhon SkyWalk (78th floor glass tray), Jodd Fairs (trendy night market)
**Food**: Pad Thai at Thipsamai (the original), Tom Yum Goong at P'Aor, Mango sticky rice at Or Tor Kor market, Jay Fai (Michelin-starred street food, THB 1,000+ per dish — reserve 6 months ahead)
**Day Trips**: Ayutthaya UNESCO ruins (1.5 hours north by train/bus), Kanchanaburi (Bridge on the River Kwai, waterfalls), Pattaya beach resort (1.5 hours south)

## Getting Around
- **BTS Skytrain**: Elevated rail covering Sukhumvit and Silom corridors; efficient; Rabbit card recommended
- **MRT Subway**: Covers additional areas including Chatuchak, Chinatown
- **Tuk-tuks**: Iconic but negotiate before (or use Grab Tuk-tuk for metered version)
- **Chao Phraya Express Boat**: Scenic and cheap (THB 15–40); connects riverside temples
- **Grab**: Essential app for reliable metered rides; avoids taxi negotiation issues
- **SkyNest Car Rental**: Available near Suvarnabhumi airport; note: traffic in Bangkok is very heavy; Grab strongly recommended for city travel

## Currency
Thai Baht (THB). USD 1 ≈ THB 35 (2026). ATMs widely available (THB 220 foreign fee standard). Supermarkets and malls accept cards; street food is cash only.

---

## Practical Tips
- Never disrespect the monarchy — it is illegal in Thailand (lèse-majesté)
- Remove shoes before entering temples; cover shoulders and knees
- The wai (pressed palms greeting) — acknowledge with a smile and slight nod
- Bargaining is expected at markets; start at 50% of asking price and negotiate pleasantly
- Street food is safe and outstanding — eat where the locals eat
