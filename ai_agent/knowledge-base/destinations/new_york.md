# New York City — SkyNest Destination Guide

## Overview
New York City is the city that never sleeps — five boroughs, 8 million people, 800 languages, and more world-class museums, restaurants, and live performances than most countries combined. Whether you want Broadway, the best pizza on Earth, fine dining at Per Se, or sunrise from the Brooklyn Bridge, NYC delivers.

## Hub Airport
**John F. Kennedy International (JFK)** — Terminal 7 for SkyNest arrivals. AirTrain to Jamaica LIRR/Subway: $8.25 + subway/LIRR fare. Taxi to Midtown Manhattan: flat rate $70 + tolls + tip (~$85 total). Uber/Lyft: $45–70 typical.

## Best Time to Visit
| Season | Months | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Spring | Apr – Jun | Beautiful, comfortable; Central Park blooms |
| Autumn (Best) | Sep – Nov | Ideal weather; colours in Central Park; less humid |
| Summer | Jun – Aug | Hot, humid; open-air events, rooftop bars peak season |
| Winter | Dec – Feb | Cold; iconic Christmas (Rockefeller tree, skating); New Year's Eve Times Square |

## Must-See Attractions

**Iconic NYC**: Statue of Liberty + Ellis Island (book ferry weeks ahead), Central Park (843 acres), Empire State Building, Brooklyn Bridge (walk across), Times Square, High Line elevated park
**World-Class Museums**: Metropolitan Museum of Art (Met — free suggested donation), MoMA, American Museum of Natural History, Whitney Museum, Guggenheim
**Neighbourhoods**: Manhattan (Midtown, SoHo, Tribeca, Harlem, Upper West Side), Brooklyn (DUMBO, Williamsburg, Park Slope), Astoria (Queens — best food diversity)
**Food**: Bagels and lox (Russ & Daughters), New York slice pizza (Joe's Pizza), pastrami sandwich (Katz's Deli), oysters at Grand Central Oyster Bar, omakase in the East Village
**Entertainment**: Broadway shows (book 6+ months for top shows), jazz at the Village Vanguard, comedy at Carolines, rooftop bars with skyline views

---

## Getting Around
- **Subway**: 24/7 operation; $2.90 per ride; buy OMNY card or use contactless credit card
- **Buses**: Good for crosstown trips; slow in traffic
- **Walking**: Most iconic way to see Manhattan; numbered streets make navigation easy
- **Citi Bike (bike share)**: Great for Hudson River Greenway and flat areas
- **Taxis / Uber**: Reliable; surge pricing during peak hours
- **SkyNest Car Rental**: Available at JFK; driving in Manhattan strongly discouraged (traffic, parking)

---

## Currency
US Dollar (USD). Cards universally accepted; tipping (18–20% at restaurants, 15% for taxis) is culturally standard and not optional in social terms.

---

## Practical Tips
- NYC is expensive — budget $150–300/day per person for mid-range experience
- Power outlets: 120V, Type A/B (two flat pins); bring adapters for UK/European/Indian plugs
- Safety has dramatically improved; standard city awareness is sufficient
- Tourist Information Centers at Midtown (810 7th Ave) and City Hall area
